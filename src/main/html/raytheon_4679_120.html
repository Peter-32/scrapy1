Our latest company video is about our people, our technology, our vision, and the values we hold dear at Raytheon. Click here to view: https://youtu.be/qvbCgKpkEds

Raytheon Intelligence, Information and Services (IIS) is currently seeking a Sr Systems Engineer I in support of the North American Aerospace Defense Command (NORAD) Integrated Space Support Contract (NISSC) program. NISSC provides accurate, timely and unambiguous warning and attack assessment of Air, Missile and Space threats with daily visibility of National Command Authority leaders. This position involves working in a team environment supporting all phases of Systems Engineering development including concept development, requirements, functional analysis, design reviews, implementation, test, verification and validation. The Sr Systems Engineer I leverages multiple skill sets in areas such as Missile Defense, Radar and RF applications, embedded computing and networking.

Clearance Requirements:
US Citizen with the ability to obtain a Secret Level security clearance

Responsibilities and scope include:

  • Perform systems engineering activities which may include CONOPs development, requirements development and management, systems architecture, system design, interface definition and control, integration and test to include test planning and procedure development in support of assigned programs

  • Work with customers and represent the engineering technical viewpoint

  • Develop, maintain and ensure compliance for system and subsystem requirements

  • Provide support to a cross-functional team throughout the lifecycle of a development program

  • Prepare for and support both internal and customer design reviews

  • Communicate effectively with the cross-functional engineering team

  • Perform responsibilities independently with limited supervision


Required Skills:

  • Experience in systems, software or hardware engineering, providing demonstrable technical expertise & value to programs, commensurate with current grade and role

  • Good written and oral communication skills

  • Experience operating within an organization governed by company policies & procedures

  • Experience on teams who design, develop, integrate, and field systems

  • Experience supporting the development of technical baselines (requirements/architecture) and Engineering Change Requests

  • Experience applying technical standards, principles, theories, concepts and techniques to solve problems.

  • Experience performing technical Analysis of Alternatives (AoA) and/or Trade Studies, performing Risk and Opportunity analyses, and tracking and mitigating risks

Desired Skills:

  • Familiarity with the existing Integrated Tactical Warning/Attack Assessment (ITW/AA) aerospace defense Systems, and their policies and procedures

  • Experience in development, integration, and test of large scale complex Mainframe systems

  • Experience with the current NCMC-ITW/AA Legacy Space maintained systems that includes CMP, DECON, SDS, and SPADOC

  • Experience with program metrics: development, financial, performance

  • Experience in capture management: creating/reviewing RFPs, SOWs, BOEs, tech volumes, proposals

  • Experience ensuring program progress via formal risk & opportunity management

  • Experience using DOORS, Rhapsody, MagicDraw, or other requirements management and system architecture tools

  • Experience mentoring engineers of all grade levels

  • Familiarity with Raytheon company policies & procedures

  • Active Secret clearance


Education Requirements:
4 years with a BS/BA in Engineering, Science, Technology, Mathematics or related STEM degree; or 2 years with MS/MA in Engineering, Science, Technology, Mathematics or related STEM degree

Business Unit Profile

Raytheon Intelligence, Information and Services delivers innovative technology to make the world a safer place. Our expertise in cyber, analytics and automation allow us to reach beyond what others think is possible to underpin national security and give our global customers unique solutions to solve the most pressing modern challenges -- from the cyber domain to automated operations, and from intelligent transportation and training solutions to creating clear insight from large volumes of data. IIS operates at nearly 550 sites in 80 countries, and is headquartered in Dulles, Virginia. The business area generated $6.2 billion in 2018 revenues. As a global business, our leaders must have the ability to understand, embrace and operate in a multicultural world -- in the marketplace and the workplace. We strive to hire people who reflect our communities and embrace diversity and inclusion to advance our culture, develop our employees, and grow our business.
